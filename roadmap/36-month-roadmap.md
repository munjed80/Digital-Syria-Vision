## Executive Summary

A 36-month (3-year) roadmap that takes the programme from pilot to
national-scale operation. All figures are **planning scenarios** consistent
with `docs/canonical-metrics-and-assumptions.md`. The roadmap is sequenced by
governorate, not by single national big-bang launch.

---

## Year 1 — Foundation and pilot

See `roadmap/12-month-roadmap.md`. End state:

- 3 governorates piloting; 15 priority services live in pilot.
- 1 — 1.5 million NDID enrolments (scenario).
- 5 policies in use; Citizen Rights Charter ratified.
- SY-CERT 24/7 + first independent audit.

---

## Year 2 — Scale to additional governorates

| Track | Year-2 outcome |
|---|---|
| Coverage | 6 — 8 governorates live (sequenced by readiness; e.g. Damascus → Damascus Countryside → Latakia → Tartus → Homs → Aleppo as conditions allow). |
| Services | 30 priority services live in covered governorates. |
| NDID | Cumulative enrolment scenario: 4–6 million citizens. |
| Assisted channels | Network of assisted-digital service centres in every covered governorate (target ratio: at least 1 centre per 50,000 citizens in covered areas). |
| Payments | GPG live for the full priority service list in covered governorates. |
| Open data | 50+ open datasets published. |
| Targets | First measurable 50% reduction in in-person visits for selected priority services (target from canonical metrics). |
| Cybersecurity | Government Cybersecurity Baseline Standard v1.0 in force; second independent audit. |
| Disaster recovery | Live DR failover drill against the secondary site at least twice. |
| Sanctions resilience | Sanctions-impact register reviewed every quarter; mitigation plan updated. |

---

## Year 3 — National coverage and consolidation

| Track | Year-3 outcome |
|---|---|
| Coverage | All accessible governorates live (subject to security and connectivity conditions). For governorates where security and connectivity conditions do not yet meet the minimum operational requirements, the rollout is reported honestly as "deferred", not as fictional rollout. |
| Services | 50+ priority services live nation-wide. |
| NDID | Cumulative enrolment scenario: 8–10 million citizens. |
| Refugee and IDP track | Re-enrolment programme for returnees, with biometric de-duplication. |
| Targets | Measurable 60% reduction in average processing time for selected priority services. |
| UN E-Gov ranking | Visible improvement in the next UN E-Government Development Index report (target: progressing toward Top 70 over 5-year horizon, not Year 3). |
| Open data | Open Data Policy enforced; 100+ datasets. |
| Workforce | Government IT workforce expanded with structured training programme; women and youth participation tracked and published. |
| Audit | Third independent audit; published summary. |

---

## What is explicitly NOT promised by Year 3

- "Top 70 UN ranking" is **not** a 3-year deliverable — it is a 5-year target.
- "100% of all government services online" is **not** a 3-year deliverable — only the priority list.
- "95% NDID coverage" is **not** the 3-year scenario; the scenario is 8–10 million citizens (about 60–70% of the adult population).

---

## Risks that may slow this roadmap

| Risk | Effect on roadmap |
|---|---|
| Sanctions tightening on procurement | +3 to +6 months on hardware/HSM-dependent milestones. |
| Currency volatility | Higher unit cost; fewer enrolment centres for the same budget. |
| Electricity instability | Slower onboarding of citizens via digital channels; higher reliance on assisted centres. |
| Connectivity gaps in rural areas | Slower NDID enrolment in those areas; SMS/USSD fallback becomes critical. |
| Security incident | One major incident can pause rollout for 30–90 days while SY-CERT and the affected ministry investigate. |
