## Vision Statement

Syria will establish a sovereign, secure, and citizen-centric digital government by 2030 — one that delivers transparent, efficient, and accessible public services to all Syrians, regardless of location or circumstance.

---

## The Challenge

Syria faces a unique and urgent digital transformation imperative:

- **Fragmented government systems** with no interoperability between ministries
- **Paper-based processes** creating inefficiency, corruption risk, and citizen burden
- **Lack of digital identity** infrastructure hindering service delivery
- **No sovereign cloud** — government data stored insecurely or abroad
- **Absent cybersecurity posture** — no national CERT, no incident response capability
- **Low public trust** in government services and data handling

---

## The Opportunity

The reconstruction period offers a generational opportunity to **build modern digital infrastructure from the ground up** — bypassing decades of legacy technical debt that constrain more established nations.

Key opportunities:
- Leapfrog legacy systems with cloud-native, API-first architecture
- Establish digital identity for all citizens simultaneously
- Build unified payment infrastructure for government services
- Create transparent, auditable government operations from day one

---

## The Solution: Digital Syria Vision

The program delivers five foundational pillars:

### Pillar 1: Digital Identity and Citizen Services
Every Syrian citizen receives a secure digital identity (National Digital ID — NDID). Citizens access all government services through a single unified portal with seamless authentication.

### Pillar 2: Government Cloud Sovereignty (SyriaGovCloud)
A sovereign government cloud infrastructure hosted within Syria, providing secure, scalable, and resilient hosting for all government applications and data.

### Pillar 3: Interoperability and Data Exchange
A national API gateway connects all ministries, enabling secure, standardized data exchange. Eliminates duplicate data entry and enables citizen-centered service delivery.

### Pillar 4: Cybersecurity and National CERT
Establishment of Syria's Computer Emergency Response Team (SY-CERT) and a national cybersecurity framework protecting government and critical infrastructure.

### Pillar 5: Digital Economy and Payment Infrastructure
A unified government payment gateway enabling online payment for all government services, integrated with the banking sector and supporting financial inclusion.

## Governance

The program is governed by the **National Digital Transformation Council** chaired by the Prime Minister, with operational oversight by the **Ministry of Communications and Technology** and technical delivery through the newly established **Digital Government Authority (DGA)**.

---

## Immediate Next Steps (100 Days)

1. Establish the Digital Government Authority and appoint leadership
2. Launch SyriaGovCloud infrastructure procurement
3. Begin National Digital ID pilot in Damascus with 50,000 citizens
4. Deploy unified citizen portal (beta)
5. Establish SY-CERT with international partnership support
6. Enact Digital Government Framework Law
